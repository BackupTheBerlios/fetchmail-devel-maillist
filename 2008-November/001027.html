Bernhard Boxhorn suggests to record sender (or other
headers, perhaps Subject) in the
oversized-messages-kept/deleted mail. Should be doable
for POP3, we must have the header when we see a reject
of MAIL FROM:<foo at bar.example> SIZE=123456789 else we
wouldn't have the envelope sender, quote:

"So, this remains as feature request, when set the
--limitflush option then headers are downloaded after
realizing that the mail exceeds the limitflush size,
deletes the mail and informs the recipient (btw, why
not the sender) that sender xyz at abc.com sent am mail
with X MB, this was 
bigger than $limitflush and the mail got deleted.
This would save the bandwith to download and then
reject the Mail and as well we could turn on
softbounce=yes for protecting us a bit when doing
configuration errors."

(softbounce referring to Postfix's soft_bounce feature.)
