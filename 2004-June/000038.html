R.O. Blättner wrote:
> I'm used to NOT reinstall every new distribution release on my boxes.
>
> And I usually grapped every new fetchmail tarball from ESR and could
> compile it like a charme although I've somtimes not the cutting edge
> of generation software installed.

You'll still be able to compile the released tarball just fine; you don't 
need that list of tools to do that.  Autoconf and the rest are necessary 
for going from what's in the code repository to what you see in the 
release tarball.  But after that, none of that stuff is necessary.

In other words, if you only work from releases, nothing will change for 
you.  But now we have the ability to get involved in the process before 
release, and that's where autoconf et al come in.
