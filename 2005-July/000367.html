On 14/07/05, perry hutchison <phutchis at beaverton.windriver.com> wrote:
> 
> If it really can't be downloaded, I suppose those are the only
> choices (and the skip case will eventually require manual
> intervention to delete the accumulated trash so the mailbox
> does not go over quota).
> 
> If it's just a matter of "don't know what to do with it" (e.g. bad
> address), I'd lean toward forwarding it to Postmaster as a default
> action.

The trouble is, in this case (and most of the other edge cases that
cause a similar problem) the email isn't valid (according to the
RFCs).  Trying to forward it is likely to run into problems as the
email is broken in some way.

I suppose it could be forwarded to the postmaster as a plain text (or
RFC-822) attachment, and let them worry about it.

Anyway, looking back at the original message:

> gethostbyname failed for ip7133120
> fetchmail: SMTP-error: 501 <perry.kimtl at emb_tunis.intl.tn>: malformed
> address: _tunis.intl.tn> may not follow <perry.kimtl at emb

That's not fetchmail, it's the SMTP server complaining.  Best bet here
IMO would be to define 501 as one of the spam codes so the message is
auto-deleted.
