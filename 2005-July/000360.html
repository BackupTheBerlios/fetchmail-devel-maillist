On 14/07/05, Nico Golde <nico at ngolde.de> wrote:
> Hi today I got this:
> 
> Fetchmail stop completely when it encounters an email with an underscore in
> the domain name. That is, it does not just skip the message - it fails to
> continue fetching other mails from this and other mail hosts.
> 
> A spam mail resulted in such a DOS on my box with the following "fatal" error:
> 
> gethostbyname failed for ip7133120
> fetchmail: SMTP-error: 501 <perry.kimtl at emb_tunis.intl.tn>: malformed
> address: _tunis.intl.tn> may not follow <perry.kimtl at emb
> 
> When I deleted the offending email from the POP3 server, fetchmail
> served me again

Possibly related to the fact that according to RFC 1035 the use of "_"
is not valid in the domain part of a name.  The only valid characters
are letters, numbers and the hyphen.

http://www.faqs.org/rfcs/rfc1035.html

Not that it helps you I know, but at least it gives you a pointer as
to where the problem may lie (beyond the stupidity of the person who's
responsible for that domain, and the software that lets them publish
the DNS record).
