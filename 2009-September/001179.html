It appears that the antispam option only applies to
results from MAIL FROM.

It would be desirable to have it also apply to RCPT TO:

The reasoning here is that the spam decision is not
completed until I know who you want to send to.
For example I may have a postmaster account that allows
non-resolvable domains and allows me to help
troubleshoot nameserver problems. All other accounts on
my server reject non-resolvable domains.

I can't imagine I am special in any way.

Consider this smtp exchange between fetchmail and my
local sendmail:

SMTP> MAIL FROM:<tenak at 123-4f47685e57d> BODY=7BIT SIZE=1459
SMTP< 250 2.1.0 <tenak at 123-4f47685e57d>... Sender ok
SMTP> RCPT TO:<a_valid_user at localhost>
SMTP< 553 5.1.8 <a_valid_user at localhost>... Domain of
sender address tenak at 123-4f47685e57d does not exist

As you can see, I'm reporting that I don't like the
sender, but only after they tell me who they want to
send to.

At the moment, to force cleaning out those dead
messages from my pop accounts, I use the "set no
softbounce" option. That's really dangerous as there's
other possible permanent errors where I would choose to
keep the mail in the pop account. Using antispam would
allow me to choose.

I'm not a fetchmail or sendmail guru. I realise I could
just be doing something wrong. I'm happy to discuss
this with anyone.
